Mathematical Concepts
and Skills (MCS)

LCA Mathematical Applications

MCS.1

Reason mathematically about problems
Students should be able to:
a.Make sense of a given problem and represent it using mathematics
b.Apply knowledge and skills to solve a problem, including decomposing it into manageable parts and/or simplifying it using appropriate assumptions
c.Interpret and justify their solution in terms of the original problem and communicate their findings mathematically

MCS.2

Reason mathematically about problems
Students should be able to:
a.Perform calculations on positive and negative numbers: addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, square roots, and positive whole number powers
b.Use the order of arithmetic operations, including the use of brackets
c.Present answers to the degree of accuracy required (nearest whole number, nearest thousand, two decimal places)
d.Use appropriate units and convert between them (mm, cm, m, km, seconds, minutes, hours, days, €k, €million, degrees)
e.Flexibly convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
f.Use and understand ratio and proportion

MCS.3

Investigate 2D and 3D shapes
Students should be able to:
a.Draw and interpret scaled diagrams, using geometric tools (ruler, straight edge, set square, protractor, compass)
b.Draw and interpret nets, including rectangular solids and cylinders
c.Find the perimeter and area of 2D shapes (combinations of discs, triangles, and rectangles)
d.Find the volume and surface area of 3D shapes (rectangular solids and cylinders)
e.Recognise and use angle facts: at a point, on a straight line, and in squares, rectangles, parallelograms, and triangles
f.Apply the theorem of Pythagoras to solve simple problems

MCS.4

Explore relationships and expressions
Students should be able to:
a.Evaluate expressions given the value of variables
b.Represent linear relationships in tables, graphs, and generalised expressions (expressed in words)
c.Select and use suitable strategies for finding solutions to problems involving linear relationships (graphic, numeric, trial and improvement, working backwards)

MCS.5

Carry out a statistical investigation
Students should be able to:
a.Generate a statistical question
b.Plan and implement a method to generate and/or source unbiased, representative data
c.Select, draw, and interpret appropriate graphical displays (bar charts, pie charts, trend graphs, histograms)
d.Select, calculate, and interpret summary statistics: measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and spread (range)
e.Evaluate the effectiveness of different graphical displays in representing data
f.Discuss misconceptions and misuses of statistics
