Mathematical Concepts and Skills (MCS)

LCA Mathematical Applications

MCS.1

Reason mathematically about problems

Students should be able to:

1aMake sense of a given problem and represent it using mathematics
1bApply knowledge and skills to solve a problem, including decomposing it into manageable parts and/or simplifying it using appropriate assumptions
1cInterpret and justify their solution in terms of the original problem and communicate their findings mathematically

MCS.2

Numerical proficiency and unit management

Students should be able to:

2aPerform calculations on positive and negative numbers: addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, square roots, and positive whole number powers
2bUse the order of arithmetic operations, including the use of brackets
2cPresent answers to the degree of accuracy required (nearest whole number, nearest thousand, two decimal places)
2dUse appropriate units and convert between them (mm, cm, m, km, seconds, minutes, hours, days, €k, €million, degrees)
2eFlexibly convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
2fUse and understand ratio and proportion

MCS.3

Investigate 2D and 3D shapes

Students should be able to:

3aDraw and interpret scaled diagrams, using geometric tools (ruler, straight edge, set square, protractor, compass)
3bDraw and interpret nets, including rectangular solids and cylinders
3cFind the perimeter and area of 2D shapes (combinations of discs, triangles, and rectangles)
3dFind the volume and surface area of 3D shapes (rectangular solids and cylinders)
3eRecognise and use angle facts: at a point, on a straight line, and in squares, rectangles, parallelograms, and triangles
3fApply the theorem of Pythagoras to solve simple problems

MCS.4

Explore relationships and expressions

Students should be able to:

4aEvaluate expressions given the value of variables
4bRepresent linear relationships in tables, graphs, and generalised expressions (expressed in words)
4cSelect and use suitable strategies for finding solutions to problems involving linear relationships (graphic, numeric, trial and improvement, working backwards)

MCS.5

Carry out a statistical investigation

Students should be able to:

5aGenerate a statistical question
5bPlan and implement a method to generate and/or source unbiased, representative data
5cSelect, draw, and interpret appropriate graphical displays (bar charts, pie charts, trend graphs, histograms)
5dSelect, calculate, and interpret summary statistics: measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and spread (range)
5eEvaluate the effectiveness of different graphical displays in representing data
5fDiscuss misconceptions and misuses of statistics
