Junior Cycle Mathematics

Learning Outcomes by Strand

Unifying Strand

Building blocks

Students should be able to:

U.1recall and demonstrate understanding of the fundamental concepts and procedures that underpin each strand
U.2apply the procedures associated with each strand accurately, effectively, and appropriately
U.3recognise that equality is a relationship in which two mathematical expressions have the same value

Unifying Strand

Representation

Students should be able to:

U.4represent a mathematical situation in a variety of different ways, including: numerically, algebraically, graphically, physically, in words; and to interpret, analyse, and compare such representations

Unifying Strand

Connections

Students should be able to:

U.5make connections within and between strands
U.6make connections between mathematics and the real world

Unifying Strand

Problem solving

Students should be able to:

U.7make sense of a given problem, and if necessary mathematise a situation
U.8apply their knowledge and skills to solve a problem, including decomposing it into manageable parts and/or simplifying it using appropriate assumptions

Unifying Strand

Problem solving (cont.)

Students should be able to:

U.9interpret their solution to a problem in terms of the original question
U.10evaluate different possible solutions to a problem, including evaluating the reasonableness of the solutions, and exploring possible improvements and/or limitations of the solutions (if any)

Unifying Strand

Generalisation, proof & communication

Students should be able to:

U.11generate general mathematical statements or conjectures based on specific instances
U.12generate and evaluate mathematical arguments and proofs
U.13communicate mathematics effectively: justify their reasoning, interpret their results, explain their conclusions, and use the language and notation of mathematics to express mathematical ideas precisely

Number Strand

N.1: Number representation & operations

Students should be able to:

N.1arepresent the operations of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division in N, Z, and Q using models including the number line, decomposition, and accumulating groups of equal size
N.1bperform the operations of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division and understand the relationship between these operations and the properties: commutative, associative and distributive in N, Z, and Q and in R\Q, including operating on surds

Number Strand

N.1: Index form & generalisations

Students should be able to:

N.1cexplore numbers written as a² (in index form) so that they can:
N.1c.iflexibly translate between whole numbers and index representation of numbers
N.1c.iiuse and apply generalisations such as aᵖaᵠ = aᵖ⁺ᵠ; (aᵖ)/(aᵠ) = aᵖ⁻ᵠ; (aᵖ)ᵠ = aᵖᵠ; and n¹/² = √n, for a ∈ ℤ, and p, q, p-q, √n ∈ ℕ and for a, b, √n ∈ ℝ, and p, q ∈ ℚ

Number Strand

N.1: More index form & other concepts

Students should be able to:

N.1c.iiiuse and apply generalisations such as a⁰ = 1; aᵖ/ᵠ = √ᵠaᵖ = (√ᵠa)ᵖ; a⁻ᵠ = 1/(aᵖ); (ab)ᵖ = aᵖbᵖ; and (a/b)ᵖ = (aᵖ)/(bᵖ), for a, b ∈ ℝ; p, q ∈ ℤ; and r ∈ ℚ
N.1c.ivgeneralise numerical relationships involving operations involving numbers written in index form
N.1dcalculate and interpret factors (including the highest common factor), multiples (including the lowest common multiple), and prime numbers

Number Strand

N.1: Accuracy & scientific notation

Students should be able to:

N.1epresent numerical answers to the degree of accuracy specified, for example, correct to the nearest hundred, to two decimal places, or to three significant figures
N.1fconvert the number p in decimal form to the form a × 10ⁿ, where 1 ≤ a < 10, n ∈ ℤ, p ∈ ℚ, and p ≥ 1 and 0 < p < 1

Number Strand

N.2: Equivalent representations

Students should be able to:

N.2aflexibly convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
N.2buse and understand ratio and proportion
N.2csolve money-related problems including those involving bills, VAT, profit or loss, % profit or loss (on the cost price), cost price, selling price, compound interest for not more than 3 years, income tax (standard rate only), net pay (including other deductions of specified amounts), value for money calculations and judgements, mark up (profit as a % of cost price), margin (profit as a % of selling price)

Number Strand

N.3: Proportionality

Students should be able to:

N.3ause absolute and relative comparison where appropriate
N.3bsolve problems involving proportionality including those involving currency conversion and those involving average speed, distance, and time

Number Strand

N.4: Numerical patterns & N.5: Sets

Students should be able to:

N.4analyse numerical patterns in different ways, including making out tables and graphs, and continue such patterns
N.5aunderstand the concept of a set as a well-defined collection of elements, and that set equality is a relationship where two sets have the same elements
N.5bdefine sets by listing their elements, if finite (including in a 2-set or 3-set Venn diagram), or by generating rules that define them

Number Strand

N.5: Set operations

Students should be able to:

N.5cuse and understand suitable set notation and terminology, including null set, ∅, subset, ⊂, complement, element, ∈, universal set, cardinal number, #, intersection, ∩, union, ∪, set difference, \, N, Z, Q, R, and R\Q
N.5dperform the operations of intersection and union on 2 sets and on 3 sets, set difference, and complement, including the use of brackets to define the order of operations
N.5einvestigate whether the set operations of intersection, union, and difference are commutative and/or associative

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT1 & GT2: Units & 2D/3D shapes

Students should be able to:

GT1calculate, interpret, and apply units of measure and time
GT2adraw and interpret scaled diagrams
GT2bdraw and interpret nets of rectangular solids, prisms (polygonal bases), cylinders
GT2cfind the perimeter and area of plane figures made from combinations of discs, triangles, and rectangles, including relevant operations involving pi

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT2: Volume & surface area

Students should be able to:

GT2dfind the volume of rectangular solids, cylinders, triangular-based prisms, spheres, and combinations of these, including relevant operations involving pi
GT2efind the surface area and curved surface area (as appropriate) of rectangular solids, cylinders, triangular-based prisms, spheres, and combinations of these

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT3: Geometry proofs - constructions

Students should be able to:

GT3aperform constructions 1 to 15 in Geometry for Post-Primary School Mathematics (constructions 3 and 7 at HL only)
GT3brecall and use the concepts, axioms, theorems, corollaries and converses, specified in Geometry for Post-Primary School Mathematics (section 9 for OL and section 10 for HL)
GT3b.iaxioms 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5
GT3b.iitheorems 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 10, 13, 14, 15 and 11, 12, 19, and appropriate converses, including relevant operations involving square roots

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT3: Geometry proofs - terms & proofs

Students should be able to:

GT3b.iiicorollaries 3, 4 and 1, 2, 5 and appropriate converses
GT3cuse and explain the terms: theorem, proof, axiom, corollary, converse, and implies
GT3dcreate and evaluate proofs of geometrical propositions
GT3edisplay understanding of the proofs of theorems 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 10, 14, 15, and 13, 19; and of corollaries 3, 4, and 1, 2, 5 (full formal proofs are not examinable)

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT4, GT5 & GT6

Students should be able to:

GT4evaluate and use trigonometric ratios (sin, cos, and tan, defined in terms of right-angled triangles) and their inverses, involving angles between 0° and 90° at integer values and in decimal form
GT5afind and interpret: distance, midpoint, slope, point of intersection, and slopes of parallel and perpendicular lines
GT5bdraw graphs of line segments and interpret such graphs in context, including discussing the rate of change (slope) and the y intercept

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT5 & GT6

Students should be able to:

GT5cfind and interpret the equation of a line in the form y = mx + c; y - y₁ = m(x - x₁) and ax + by + c = 0 (for a, b, c, m, x₁, y₁ ∈ ℚ); including finding the slope, the y intercept, and other points on the line
GT6arecognise and draw the image of points and objects under translation, central symmetry, axial symmetry, and rotation
GT6bdraw the axes of symmetry in shapes

Algebra & Functions

AF1: Patterns & relationships

Students should be able to:

AF1arepresent these patterns and relationships in tables and graphs
AF1bgenerate a generalised expression for linear and quadratic patterns in words and algebraic expressions and fluently convert between each representation
AF1ccategorise patterns as linear, non-linear, quadratic, and exponential (doubling and tripling) using their defining characteristics as they appear in the different representations

Algebra & Functions

AF2 & AF3: Variables & expressions

Students should be able to:

AF2agenerate and interpret expressions in which letters stand for numbers
AF2bfind the value of expressions given the value of the variables
AF2cuse the concept of equality to generate and interpret equations
AF3a.iadd, subtract and simplify linear expressions in one or more variables with coefficients in Q

Algebra & Functions

AF3: Simplifying expressions

Students should be able to:

AF3a.iiadd, subtract and simplify quadratic expressions in one variable with coefficients in Z
AF3a.iiiadd, subtract and simplify expressions of the form a/(bx + c), where a, b, c ∈ ℤ
AF3b.imultiply expressions of the form a(bx + cy + d); a(bx² + cx + d); and ax(bx² + cx + d), where a, b, c, d ∈ ℤ
AF3b.iimultiply expressions of the form (ax + b)(cx + d) and (ax + b)(cx² + dx + e), where a, b, c, d, e ∈ ℤ

Algebra & Functions

AF3: Division & factorisation

Students should be able to:

AF3cdivide quadratic and cubic expressions by linear expressions, where all coefficients are integers and there is no remainder
AF3d.iflexibly convert between the factorised and expanded forms of algebraic expressions of the form: axy, where a ∈ ℤ
AF3d.iiflexibly convert between the factorised and expanded forms of algebraic expressions of the form: axy + byz, where a, b ∈ ℤ
AF3d.iiiflexibly convert between the factorised and expanded forms of algebraic expressions of the form: sx - ty + tx - sy, where s, t ∈ ℤ

Algebra & Functions

AF3: More factorisation & AF4 strategies

Students should be able to:

AF3d.ivflexibly convert between the factorised and expanded forms of algebraic expressions of the form: dx² + bx; x² + bx + c; and ax² + bx + c, where b, c, d ∈ ℤ and a ∈ ℕ
AF3d.vflexibly convert between the factorised and expanded forms of algebraic expressions of the form: x² - a² and a²x² - b²y², where a, b ∈ ℤ
AF4aselect and use suitable strategies for finding solutions to: linear equations in one variable with coefficients in Q and solutions in Z or in Q
AF4bselect and use suitable strategies for finding solutions to: quadratic equations in one variable with coefficients and solutions in Z or coefficients in Q and solutions in R

Algebra & Functions

AF4 & AF5: Equations & functions

Students should be able to:

AF4cselect and use suitable strategies for finding solutions to: simultaneous linear equations in two variables with coefficients and solutions in Z or in Q
AF4dselect and use suitable strategies for finding solutions to: linear inequalities in one variable of the form g(x) < k, and graph the solution sets on the number line for x ∈ ℕ, ℤ, and R
AF5generate quadratic equations given integer roots

Algebra & Functions

AF6 & AF7: Functions

Students should be able to:

AF6apply the relationship between operations and an understanding of the order of operations including brackets and exponents to change the subject of a formula
AF7ademonstrate understanding of the concept of a function
AF7brepresent and interpret functions in different ways—graphically (for x ∈ ℕ, ℤ, and ℝ, [continuous functions only], as appropriate], diagrammatically, in words, and algebraically — using the language and notation of functions (domain, range, co-domain, f(x) =, and y =)

Algebra & Functions

AF7: Functions continued

Students should be able to:

AF7cuse graphical methods to find and interpret approximate solutions of equations such as f(x) = g(x) and approximate solution sets of inequalities such as f(x) < g(x)
AF7dmake connections between the shape of a graph and the story of a phenomenon, including identifying and interpreting maximum and minimum points

Statistics & Probability

SP1 & SP2: Experiments & probability

Students should be able to:

SP1agenerate a sample space for an experiment in a systematic way, including tree diagrams for successive events and two-way tables for independent events
SP1buse the fundamental principle of counting to solve authentic problems
SP2ademonstrate understanding that probability is a measure on a scale of 0-1 of how likely an event (including an everyday event) is to occur
SP2buse the principle that, in the case of equally likely outcomes, the probability of an event is given by the number of outcomes of interest divided by the total number of outcomes

Statistics & Probability

SP2 & SP3: Probability & investigation

Students should be able to:

SP2cuse relative frequency as an estimate of the probability of an event, given experimental data, and recognise that increasing the number of times an experiment is repeated generally leads to progressively better estimates of its theoretical probability
SP3agenerate a statistical question
SP3bplan and implement a method to generate and/or source unbiased, representative data, and present this data in a frequency table
SP3cclassify data (categorical, numerical)

Statistics & Probability

SP3: Graphical displays & statistics

Students should be able to:

SP3dselect, draw and interpret appropriate graphical displays of univariate data, including pie charts, bar charts, line plots, histograms (equal intervals), ordered stem and leaf plots, and ordered back-to-back stem and leaf plots
SP3eselect, calculate and interpret appropriate summary statistics to describe aspects of univariate data. Central tendency: mean (including of a grouped frequency distribution), median, mode. Variability: range
SP3fevaluate the effectiveness of different graphical displays in representing data

Statistics & Probability

SP3: Discussion & limitations

Students should be able to:

SP3gdiscuss misconceptions and misuses of statistics
SP3hdiscuss the assumptions and limitations of conclusions drawn from sample data or graphical/numerical summaries of data
