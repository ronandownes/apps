Learning Outcomes by Strand

Student-friendly version

Unifying Strand

Building blocks

Students should be able to:

U.1OFFICIALrecall and demonstrate understanding of the fundamental concepts and procedures that underpin each strand
U.1STUDENTKnow, understand, explain and correctly use core maths ideas and methods
U.2OFFICIALapply the procedures associated with each strand accurately, effectively, and appropriately
U.2STUDENTUse mathematical methods accurately and appropriately
U.3OFFICIALrecognise that equality is a relationship in which two mathematical expressions have the same value
U.3STUDENTUnderstand that = means both sides have the same value

Unifying Strand

Representation & Connections

Students should be able to:

U.4OFFICIALrepresent a mathematical situation in a variety of different ways, including: numerically, algebraically, graphically, physically, in words; and to interpret, analyse, and compare such representations
U.4STUDENTShow maths in different ways (numbers, algebra, graphs, diagrams, words) and move between them
U.5OFFICIALmake connections within and between strands
U.5STUDENTSee how different parts of maths link together
U.6OFFICIALmake connections between mathematics and the real world
U.6STUDENTApply maths to real-life situations

Unifying Strand

Problem solving

Students should be able to:

U.7OFFICIALmake sense of a given problem, and if necessary mathematise a situation
U.7STUDENTUnderstand a problem and turn it into maths
U.8OFFICIALapply their knowledge and skills to solve a problem, including decomposing it into manageable parts and/or simplifying it using appropriate assumptions
U.8STUDENTBreak problems into smaller steps and solve them
U.9OFFICIALinterpret their solution to a problem in terms of the original question
U.9STUDENTCheck your answer makes sense for the original question
U.10OFFICIALevaluate different possible solutions to a problem, including evaluating the reasonableness of the solutions, and exploring possible improvements and/or limitations of the solutions (if any)
U.10STUDENTJudge whether answers are reasonable and consider other approaches

Unifying Strand

Generalisation & Communication

Students should be able to:

U.11OFFICIALgenerate general mathematical statements or conjectures based on specific instances
U.11STUDENTSpot patterns and make general rules from examples
U.12OFFICIALgenerate and evaluate mathematical arguments and proofs
U.12STUDENTBuild and check mathematical arguments
U.13OFFICIALcommunicate mathematics effectively: justify their reasoning, interpret their results, explain their conclusions, and use the language and notation of mathematics to express mathematical ideas precisely
U.13STUDENTExplain your maths clearly using correct language and notation

Number Strand

N1a-b: Arithmetic operations

Students should be able to:

N.1aOFFICIALrepresent the operations of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division in ℕ, ℤ, and ℚ using models including the number line, decomposition, and accumulating groups of equal size
N.1aSTUDENTUse models (number lines, groups, diagrams) to understand +, −, ×, ÷ with whole numbers, integers, and fractions
N.1bOFFICIALperform the operations of addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division and understand the relationship between these operations and the properties: commutative, associative and distributive in ℕ, ℤ, and ℚ and in ℝ\ℚ, including operating on surds [HL]
N.1bSTUDENTCarry out the four operations correctly and understand the rules they follow (commutative, associative, distributive) including surds [HL]

Number Strand

N1c: Index form (I-III)

Students should be able to:

N.1c.IOFFICIALflexibly translate between whole numbers and index representation of numbers
N.1c.ISTUDENTMove easily between whole numbers and index (power) form
N.1c.IIOFFICIALuse and apply generalisations such as aᵖaᵍ = aᵖ⁺ᵍ; aᵖ/aᵍ = aᵖ⁻ᵍ; (aᵖ)ᵍ = aᵖᵍ; and n¹ʹ² = √n extended to ℝ and ℚ [HL]
N.1c.IISTUDENTUse the laws of indices correctly when multiplying, dividing, or raising powers [HL: extend to all real numbers]
N.1c.IIIOFFICIAL[HL only] use and apply generalisations such as a⁰ = 1; aᵖʹᵍ = ᵍ√aᵖ; a⁻ʳ = 1/aʳ; (ab)ʳ = aʳbʳ
N.1c.IIISTUDENT[HL only] Use zero, negative, and fractional indices with understanding

Number Strand

N1c: Index form (IV-V)

Students should be able to:

N.1c.IVOFFICIALgeneralise numerical relationships involving operations involving numbers written in index form
N.1c.IVSTUDENTSpot patterns in calculations involving indices and use them to make general rules
N.1c.VOFFICIALcorrectly use the order of arithmetic and index operations including the use of brackets
N.1c.VSTUDENTUse the correct order of operations, including powers and brackets

Number Strand

N1d-f: Factors, accuracy & notation

Students should be able to:

N.1dOFFICIALcalculate and interpret factors (including the highest common factor), multiples (including the lowest common multiple), and prime numbers
N.1dSTUDENTFind and use factors, HCF, multiples, LCM, and prime numbers
N.1eOFFICIALpresent numerical answers to the degree of accuracy specified, for example, correct to the nearest hundred, to two decimal places, or to three significant figures
N.1eSTUDENTGive numerical answers to the required level of accuracy
N.1fOFFICIALconvert the number p in decimal form to the form a × 10ⁿ, where 1 ≤ a < 10, n ∈ ℤ, p ∈ ℚ and 0 < p < 1 [HL]
N.1fSTUDENTWrite numbers in standard form and interpret what that form means [HL: including small numbers]

Number Strand

N2: Rational numbers

Students should be able to:

N.2aOFFICIALflexibly convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
N.2aSTUDENTConvert confidently between fractions, decimals, and percentages
N.2bOFFICIALuse and understand ratio and proportion
N.2bSTUDENTUse ratio and proportion to compare quantities and solve problems
N.2cOFFICIALsolve money-related problems including those involving bills, VAT, profit or loss, % profit or loss, cost price, selling price, compound interest for not more than 3 years, income tax (standard rate only), net pay, value for money, mark up, margin, compound interest, extended tax [HL]
N.2cSTUDENTSolve real-life money problems involving prices, profit/loss, interest, tax, and value for money [HL: mark-up, margin, extended calculations]

Number Strand

N3-N4: Proportionality & patterns

Students should be able to:

N.3aOFFICIALuse absolute and relative comparison where appropriate
N.3aSTUDENTCompare quantities using absolute and relative comparisons
N.3bOFFICIALsolve problems involving proportionality including those involving currency conversion and those involving average speed, distance, and time
N.3bSTUDENTSolve proportional problems such as currency conversion and speed–distance–time
N.4OFFICIALanalyse numerical patterns in different ways, including making out tables and graphs, and continue such patterns
N.4STUDENTRecognise, describe, and continue number patterns using tables and graphs

Number Strand

N5a-c: Sets concepts

Students should be able to:

N.5aOFFICIALunderstand the concept of a set as a well-defined collection of elements, and that set equality is a relationship where two sets have the same elements
N.5aSTUDENTUnderstand what a set is and when two sets are equal
N.5bOFFICIALdefine sets by listing their elements, if finite (including in a 2-set or 3-set [HL] Venn diagram), or by generating rules that define them
N.5bSTUDENTDescribe sets by listing elements or using a rule [HL: 3-set Venn diagrams]
N.5cOFFICIALuse and understand suitable set notation and terminology, including null set ∅, subset ⊂, complement, element ∈, universal set, cardinal number #, intersection ∩, union ∪, set difference \, ℕ, ℤ, ℚ, ℝ, and ℝ\ℚ
N.5cSTUDENTUse correct set language and notation (∅, ⊂, ∈, ∩, ∪, \, etc.)

Number Strand

N5d-e: Set operations

Students should be able to:

N.5dOFFICIALperform the operations of intersection and union on 2 sets and on 3 sets [HL], set difference, and complement, including the use of brackets to define the order of operations
N.5dSTUDENTCarry out set operations: union, intersection, difference, and complement [HL: extend to 3 sets]
N.5eOFFICIAL[HL only] investigate whether the set operations of intersection, union, and difference are commutative and/or associative
N.5eSTUDENT[HL only] Investigate how set operations behave and what rules they follow

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT1-GT2: Shapes & solids

Students should be able to:

GT.1OFFICIALcalculate, interpret, and apply units of measure and time
GT.1STUDENTUse and interpret units of length, area, volume, mass, time, and angle correctly
GT.2aOFFICIALdraw and interpret scaled diagrams
GT.2aSTUDENTDraw and interpret scaled diagrams accurately
GT.2bOFFICIALdraw and interpret nets of rectangular solids, prisms (polygonal bases) [HL], cylinders
GT.2bSTUDENTDraw and understand nets of solids such as cuboids, prisms, and cylinders

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT2c-e: Area, volume & surface area

Students should be able to:

GT.2cOFFICIALfind the perimeter and area of plane figures made from combinations of discs, triangles, and rectangles, including relevant operations involving pi
GT.2cSTUDENTFind the perimeter and area of shapes made from basic figures
GT.2dOFFICIALfind the volume of rectangular solids, cylinders, triangular-based prisms, spheres [HL], and combinations of these
GT.2dSTUDENTFind the volume of solids such as cuboids, cylinders, and combinations [HL: prisms, spheres]
GT.2eOFFICIALfind the surface area and curved surface area (as appropriate) [HL] of rectangular solids, cylinders, triangular-based prisms, spheres [HL]
GT.2eSTUDENTFind surface area (and curved surface area where appropriate) of solids

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT3a-b: Constructions & theorems

Students should be able to:

GT.3aOFFICIALperform constructions 1 to 15 in Geometry for Post-Primary School Mathematics (constructions 3 and 7 at HL only)
GT.3aSTUDENTCarry out standard geometric constructions accurately
GT.3bOFFICIALrecall and use the concepts, axioms, theorems, corollaries and converses, specified in Geometry for Post-Primary School Mathematics (section 9 for OL and section 10 for HL)
GT.3bSTUDENTUse geometric facts, theorems, and results to solve problems

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT3c-e: Proof concepts

Students should be able to:

GT.3cOFFICIALuse and explain [HL] the terms: theorem, proof, axiom, corollary, converse, and implies
GT.3cSTUDENTUnderstand and use geometric language such as theorem, proof, and converse
GT.3dOFFICIALcreate and evaluate proofs of geometrical propositions
GT.3dSTUDENTCreate and evaluate geometric proofs
GT.3eOFFICIALdisplay understanding of the proofs of theorems 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 10, 14, 15, and 13, 19 [HL]; and of corollaries 3, 4, and 1, 2, 5 [HL] (full formal proofs are not examinable)
GT.3eSTUDENTUnderstand how key geometric proofs work, even if not writing full formal proofs

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT4-GT5a: Trigonometry & coordinate geometry

Students should be able to:

GT.4OFFICIALevaluate and use trigonometric ratios (sin, cos, and tan, defined in terms of right-angled triangles) and their inverses, involving angles between 0° and 90° at integer values and in decimal form [HL]
GT.4STUDENTUse trigonometric ratios to solve problems involving right-angled triangles
GT.5aOFFICIALfind and interpret: distance, midpoint, slope, point of intersection, and slopes of parallel and perpendicular [HL] lines
GT.5aSTUDENTFind and interpret distance, midpoint, slope, and intersections of lines

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT5b-c: Line equations

Students should be able to:

GT.5bOFFICIALdraw graphs of line segments and interpret such graphs in context, including discussing the rate of change (slope) and the y intercept
GT.5bSTUDENTDraw and interpret graphs of straight lines
GT.5cOFFICIALfind and interpret the equation of a line in the form y = mx + c; y − y₁ = m(x − x₁); and ax + by + c = 0 [HL]
GT.5cSTUDENTFind and use equations of straight lines in different forms

Geometry & Trigonometry

GT6: Transformations

Students should be able to:

GT.6aOFFICIALrecognise and draw the image of points and objects under translation, central symmetry, axial symmetry, and rotation
GT.6aSTUDENTRecognise and draw shapes after translations, reflections, rotations, and symmetries
GT.6bOFFICIALdraw the axes of symmetry in shapes
GT.6bSTUDENTIdentify and draw axes of symmetry

Algebra & Functions

AF1: Patterns & relationships

Students should be able to:

AF.1aOFFICIALrepresent these patterns and relationships in tables and graphs
AF.1aSTUDENTShow patterns using tables and graphs
AF.1bOFFICIALgenerate a generalised expression for linear and quadratic [HL] patterns in words and algebraic expressions
AF.1bSTUDENTWrite general rules for linear and quadratic [HL] patterns in words and algebra
AF.1cOFFICIALcategorise patterns as linear, non-linear, quadratic, and exponential (doubling and tripling) [HL]
AF.1cSTUDENTIdentify pattern types: linear, non-linear, quadratic, exponential [HL]

Algebra & Functions

AF2-AF3a: Expressions

Students should be able to:

AF.2aOFFICIALgenerate and interpret expressions in which letters stand for numbers
AF.2aSTUDENTWrite and understand algebraic expressions
AF.2bOFFICIALfind the value of expressions given the value of the variables
AF.2bSTUDENTSubstitute values into expressions to find answers
AF.2cOFFICIALuse the concept of equality to generate and interpret equations
AF.2cSTUDENTWrite and interpret equations
AF.3aOFFICIALadd, subtract and simplify: linear expressions; quadratic expressions in one variable; expressions of the form a/(bx+c) [HL]
AF.3aSTUDENTAdd, subtract and simplify algebraic expressions [HL: including algebraic fractions]

Algebra & Functions

AF3b: Multiplication of expressions

Students should be able to:

AF.3b.IOFFICIALmultiply expressions of the form: a(bx + cy + d); a(bx² + cx + d); and ax(bx² + cx + d)
AF.3b.ISTUDENTMultiply out brackets like a(bx + c) and ax(bx² + cx + d)
AF.3b.IIOFFICIALmultiply expressions of the form: (ax + b)(cx + d) and (ax + b)(cx² + dx + e) [HL]
AF.3b.IISTUDENTMultiply two brackets like (x + 2)(x + 3) [HL: including trinomials]

Algebra & Functions

AF3c-d: Division & factorisation

Students should be able to:

AF.3cOFFICIALdivide quadratic and cubic [HL] expressions by linear expressions, where all coefficients are integers and there is no remainder
AF.3cSTUDENTDivide quadratic and cubic [HL] expressions by linear expressions (no remainder)
AF.3dOFFICIALflexibly convert between the factorised and expanded forms of algebraic expressions: axy; axy + byz; sx − ty + tx − sy; dx² + bx; x² + bx + c; and ax² + bx + c [HL]; x² − a² and a²x² − b²y² [HL]
AF.3dSTUDENTFactorise and expand algebraic expressions fluently [HL: harder quadratics, difference of squares]

Algebra & Functions

AF4a-b: Solving equations

Students should be able to:

AF.4aOFFICIALselect and use suitable strategies for finding solutions to linear equations in one variable with coefficients in ℚ and solutions in ℤ or in ℚ [HL]
AF.4aSTUDENTSolve linear equations using appropriate methods
AF.4bOFFICIALselect and use suitable strategies for finding solutions to quadratic equations in one variable with coefficients and solutions in ℤ or coefficients in ℚ and solutions in ℝ [HL]
AF.4bSTUDENTSolve quadratic equations [HL: including irrational solutions]

Algebra & Functions

AF4c-d: Simultaneous & inequalities

Students should be able to:

AF.4cOFFICIALselect and use suitable strategies for finding solutions to simultaneous linear equations in two variables with coefficients and solutions in ℤ or in ℚ [HL]
AF.4cSTUDENTSolve simultaneous equations in two variables
AF.4dOFFICIALselect and use suitable strategies for finding solutions to linear inequalities in one variable of the form g(x) < k, and graph the solution sets on the number line
AF.4dSTUDENTSolve linear inequalities and show solutions on a number line

Algebra & Functions

AF.5OFFICIAL[HL only] generate quadratic equations given integer roots

AF.6STUDENT[HL only] Rearrange formulas to change the subject

Algebra & Functions

AF.7aOFFICIALdemonstrate understanding of the concept of a function
AF.7aSTUDENTUnderstand what a function is

AF.7bSTUDENTShow and interpret functions using graphs, diagrams, words, and algebra; use correct function notation

Algebra & Functions

AF7c-d: Graphical methods

Students should be able to:

AF.7cOFFICIALuse graphical methods to find and interpret approximate solutions of equations such as f(x) = g(x) and approximate solution sets of inequalities such as f(x) < g(x) [HL]
AF.7cSTUDENTUse graphs to find approximate solutions to equations and inequalities [HL]
AF.7dOFFICIALmake connections between the shape of a graph and the story of a phenomenon, including identifying and interpreting maximum and minimum points
AF.7dSTUDENTConnect graph shapes to real-world situations; identify maximum and minimum points

Statistics & Probability

SP1-SP2: Experiments & probability

Students should be able to:

SP.1aOFFICIALgenerate a sample space for an experiment in a systematic way, including tree diagrams for successive events and two-way tables for independent events
SP.1aSTUDENTList all possible outcomes systematically using tree diagrams and two-way tables
SP.1bOFFICIALuse the fundamental principle of counting to solve authentic problems
SP.1bSTUDENTUse counting methods to find how many ways something can happen
SP.2aOFFICIALdemonstrate understanding that probability is a measure on a scale of 0-1 of how likely an event is to occur
SP.2aSTUDENTUnderstand probability as a number from 0 to 1 measuring likelihood
SP.2bOFFICIALuse the principle that, in the case of equally likely outcomes, the probability of an event is given by the number of outcomes of interest divided by the total number of outcomes
SP.2bSTUDENTCalculate probability = favourable outcomes ÷ total outcomes

Statistics & Probability

SP2c-SP3c: Investigation

Students should be able to:

SP.2cOFFICIALuse relative frequency as an estimate of the probability of an event, given experimental data
SP.2cSTUDENTEstimate probability from experimental data (relative frequency)
SP.3aOFFICIALgenerate a statistical question
SP.3aSTUDENTAsk a good statistical question
SP.3bOFFICIALplan and implement a method to generate and/or source unbiased, representative data, and present this data in a frequency table
SP.3bSTUDENTCollect fair, representative data and organise it in a frequency table
SP.3cOFFICIALclassify data (categorical, numerical)
SP.3cSTUDENTClassify data as categorical or numerical

Statistics & Probability

SP3d-f: Graphical displays

Students should be able to:

SP.3dOFFICIALselect, draw and interpret appropriate graphical displays of univariate data, including pie charts, bar charts, line plots, histograms, ordered stem and leaf plots, and ordered back-to-back stem and leaf plots [HL]
SP.3dSTUDENTChoose, draw and interpret appropriate charts and graphs for data
SP.3eOFFICIALselect, calculate and interpret appropriate summary statistics to describe aspects of univariate data: mean (including of a grouped frequency distribution) [HL], median, mode; range
SP.3eSTUDENTCalculate and interpret mean, median, mode, and range [HL: including grouped data]
SP.3fOFFICIALevaluate the effectiveness of different graphical displays in representing data
SP.3fSTUDENTJudge which type of graph best represents the data

Statistics & Probability

SP3g-h: Discussion & limitations

Students should be able to:

SP.3gOFFICIALdiscuss misconceptions and misuses of statistics
SP.3gSTUDENTIdentify when statistics are being misused or misunderstood
SP.3hOFFICIALdiscuss the assumptions and limitations of conclusions drawn from sample data or graphical/numerical summaries of data
SP.3hSTUDENTUnderstand limitations of conclusions drawn from data
