The Circle Equation

circle radius tangent

The standard equation of a circle with centre (h, k) and radius r is:

(x − h)2 + (y − k)2 = r2

Special Cases

Special Case: Centre at Origin (h = 0, k = 0)

When the centre is at the origin, the equation simplifies to:

x2 + y2 = r2
Point on Circle Through Origin

If a point (x1, y1) lies on a circle centred at the origin:

x2 + y2 = |x1|2 + |y1|2
💡 Key insight: Because we square the coordinates, the signs don't matter! The sum of two squares is always positive.

Question

Worked Example 20.1 p. 485

The circle c has centre (0, 0) and radius 7 units.

Write down the equation of c.

Key Concept

Since the centre is at the origin, we use:

x2 + y2 = r2

The radius r = 7, so we need 72 on the right-hand side.

Solution

Step 1 Identify what we know: Centre = (0, 0) Radius r = 7
Step 2 Write the equation of a circle centred at the origin: x2 + y2 = r2
Step 3 Substitute r = 7: x2 + y2 = (7)2 x2 + y2 = 49
∴ x2 + y2 = 49
