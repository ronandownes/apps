Recognise and Use Angle Facts

This presentation covers key angle facts that are essential in Singapore Math curriculum:

  • Angles at a point sum to 360°
  • Angles on a straight line sum to 180°
  • Right angles (90°) in squares and rectangles
  • Opposite angles are equal in parallelograms
  • Angles in a triangle sum to 180°

These angle relationships are fundamental for solving geometric problems efficiently.

1

Angles at a Point

When two or more angles meet at a single point, they form a full rotation.

Sum of angles at a point = 360°

This is because a full rotation around a point completes a circle, which is 360°.

Example: If three angles meet at a point and two of them are 110° and 85°, the third angle would be:

360° - 110° - 85° = 165°

Angles at a Point

  • Angles that meet at a single point sum to 360°
  • This represents a full rotation around the point
  • Formula: ∠A + ∠B + ∠C + ... = 360°
  • Application: Finding missing angles when multiple lines meet at a point

Example: Three angles meet at a point: 110°, 85°, and x°.

Solution: x = 360 - 110 - 85 = 165°

2

Angles on a Straight Line

When angles are formed on a straight line, they always add up to 180°.

Sum of angles on a straight line = 180°

A straight line forms a straight angle, which is exactly 180°.

Example: If a straight line is divided into two angles and one is 75°, the other must be:

180° - 75° = 105°

This property is useful when solving for unknown angles in geometric figures.

Angles on a Straight Line

  • Angles that form a straight line sum to 180°
  • This is called a straight angle or supplementary angles
  • Formula: ∠A + ∠B = 180° (when on a straight line)
  • Application: Finding missing angles in linear pairs

Example: Two angles on a straight line: 75° and x°.

Solution: x = 180 - 75 = 105°

Note: Angles that add to 180° are called supplementary angles.

3

Angles in Squares and Rectangles

Squares and rectangles are quadrilaterals with specific angle properties:

  • All interior angles are right angles (90°)
  • The sum of interior angles is 360° (4 × 90°)
  • Opposite sides are parallel and equal in length
Each angle in a square/rectangle = 90°

Key difference: In a square, all sides are equal, while in a rectangle, only opposite sides are equal.

These properties help identify these shapes and solve related problems.

Angles in Squares and Rectangles

  • All interior angles are 90° (right angles)
  • Sum of interior angles = 360°
  • Opposite sides are parallel and equal
  • Diagonals bisect each other
  • Squares: All sides equal, all angles 90°
  • Rectangles: Opposite sides equal, all angles 90°

Example Application: If one angle of a rectangle is 90°, all other angles must also be 90°.

This property is used to prove shapes are rectangles or squares.

4

Angles in Parallelograms

A parallelogram is a quadrilateral with opposite sides parallel. Key angle properties:

  • Opposite angles are equal
  • Adjacent angles are supplementary (add to 180°)
  • Sum of interior angles is 360°
∠A = ∠C and ∠B = ∠D
∠A + ∠B = 180°, ∠B + ∠C = 180°, etc.

Example: In a parallelogram, if one angle is 70°, the opposite angle is also 70°, and adjacent angles are 110°.

This property helps identify parallelograms and calculate unknown angles.

Angles in Parallelograms

  • Opposite angles are equal (∠A = ∠C, ∠B = ∠D)
  • Adjacent angles are supplementary (∠A + ∠B = 180°)
  • Sum of interior angles = 360°
  • Opposite sides are parallel and equal
  • Diagonals bisect each other

Example: In parallelogram ABCD, ∠A = 70°.

Then ∠C = 70° (opposite angle), and ∠B = ∠D = 180° - 70° = 110° (adjacent angles).

5

Angles in Triangles

Triangles have three sides and three angles with this fundamental property:

Sum of angles in a triangle = 180°

This is true for all triangles, regardless of their type (equilateral, isosceles, or scalene).

Special triangle types:

  • Equilateral: All angles = 60°
  • Isosceles: Two equal angles
  • Right-angled: One angle = 90°

Example: In a triangle, if two angles are 50° and 60°, the third angle is:

180° - 50° - 60° = 70°

Angles in Triangles

  • Sum of interior angles = 180°
  • Exterior angle = sum of two opposite interior angles
  • Equilateral triangle: All sides equal, all angles = 60°
  • Isosceles triangle: Two sides equal, two base angles equal
  • Right triangle: One angle = 90°

Example: Triangle with angles 50°, 60°, and x°.

Solution: x = 180 - 50 - 60 = 70°

Exterior angle theorem: An exterior angle equals the sum of the two opposite interior angles.

6

Summary and Application

Let's review the key angle facts covered in this presentation:

  • At a point: Angles sum to 360°
  • On a straight line: Angles sum to 180°
  • In squares/rectangles: All angles are 90°
  • In parallelograms: Opposite angles equal, adjacent angles supplementary
  • In triangles: Angles sum to 180°
Problem-Solving Strategy: Identify the geometric figure and apply the appropriate angle fact to find missing angles.

These angle relationships form the foundation for more advanced geometry concepts in Singapore Math.

Summary of Angle Facts

  • Angles at a point: Sum = 360°
  • Angles on a straight line: Sum = 180° (supplementary)
  • Squares and rectangles: All angles = 90°
  • Parallelograms: Opposite angles equal, adjacent angles supplementary
  • Triangles: Sum of angles = 180°

Application in Problem Solving:

  1. Identify the geometric figure(s) in the problem
  2. Recall the relevant angle fact(s)
  3. Set up equation(s) based on the angle relationships
  4. Solve for the unknown angle(s)
  5. Check if the answer makes sense geometrically

Mastering these angle facts is essential for success in geometry and Singapore Math.
