Introduction
circle centre radius tangent
Standard equation of a circle with centre (h, k) and radius r:
(x − h)2 + (y − k)2 = r2

Special Case: Centre at Origin (h = 0, k = 0)

x2 + y2 = r2
💡 Signs don't matter when squaring — sum of squares is always positive!

Finding Radius from Point

If point (x1, y1) is on circle centred at origin:

r = √(x12 + y12)

Circle t has centre (0, 0) and contains A(−5, 0), B(5, 0) and P(−4, k).
(a) Find equation of t.   (b) Find k (where k > 0).   (c) Show triangle ABP is right-angled at P.
Solution
(a)
r = 5 (distance to A) ⟹ x2 + y2 = 25
Equation of t
(b)
(−4)2 + k2 = 25 ⟹ 16 + k2 = 25
P is on circle
k2 = 9 ⟹ k = 3
k > 0
(c)
Slope AP = 3/1 = 3    Slope BP = 3/−9 = −⅓
Find slopes
3 × (−⅓) = −1   ∴ AP ⊥ BP ✓
