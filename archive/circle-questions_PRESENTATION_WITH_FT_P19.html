The circle has centre (−3, 3) and has radius 3.

(x + 3)² + (y − 3)² = 9

Question

The circle passes through the origin (0, 0).
  • (a) Find the co-ordinates of B.
  • (b) Calculate the radius of the circle.
  • (c) Write down the equation of the circle.

By symmetry through centre: If A(−3, 4), centre is midpoint, and B is the reflection.
Centre = ((−3+x)/2, (4+y)/2)
Distance from centre to origin = distance from centre to A
Since A, origin, and B are related: B = (3, −4)
(b) Diameter = |AB| = √[(3−(−3))² + (−4−4)²] = √[36 + 64] = √100 = 10
Radius = 10/2 = 5
(c) Centre = ((−3+3)/2, (4−4)/2) = (0, 0) x² + y² = 25
(a) B(3, −4)   (b) r = 5   (c) x² + y² = 25

Q12

Question

The diagram shows the circle c. The point P(12, k), k > 0, is on the circle.
  • (a) Find the value of k.
  • (b) Show that AP is perpendicular to BP.

Solution

(a) Circle has diameter from B(−13, 0) to A(13, 0)
Centre = (0, 0), radius = 13
Equation: x² + y² = 169
Point P(12, k) is on circle: 12² + k² = 169 144 + k² = 169 k² = 25, so k = 5 (since k > 0)
(b) P = (12, 5), A = (13, 0), B = (−13, 0)
Slope of AP = (5−0)/(12−13) = 5/(−1) = −5
Slope of BP = (5−0)/(12−(−13)) = 5/25 = 1/5
Product of slopes = (−5) × (1/5) = −1
(a) k = 5   (b) Slopes multiply to −1 ∴ AP ⊥ BP

Co-ordinate Geometry: The Circle

Summary

Key Formulas

Circle centred at origin: x² + y² = r²
Circle centred at (h, k): (x − h)² + (y − k)² = r²
Midpoint formula: M = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)
Distance formula: d = √[(x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²]

Key Facts

Centre from diameter: Midpoint of diameter endpoints
Radius from diameter: Half the diameter length
Touches x-axis: radius = |k| (y-coord of centre)
Touches y-axis: radius = |h| (x-coord of centre)
Perpendicular lines: product of slopes = −1
Circumference: C = 2πr
Area: A = πr²
