Ending Digits

A square can only end in:

0, 1, 4, 5, 6, 9

So numbers ending in 2, 3, 7, 8 are never square.

Last‑Digit Mapping

0 → 0
1 → 1
2 → 4
3 → 9
5 → 5
6 → 6
7 → 9
8 → 4
9 → 1

Example: 12² = 144 → ends in 4.

Odd and Even

(2n)² = 4n² → even

(2n + 1)² = 4n² + 4n + 1 → odd

Building Squares

If you know one square, add the base and the next base.

20² = 400
21² = 400 + 20 + 21 = 441
