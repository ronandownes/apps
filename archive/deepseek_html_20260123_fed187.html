Angle Facts in LCA Maths

Leaving Certificate Applied (LCA) Mathematics requires understanding of basic angle relationships.
These facts are essential for solving geometry problems in everyday contexts.
Today we will cover:
• Angles at a point
• Angles on a straight line
• Angles in triangles
• Angles in quadrilaterals

ANGLES
1

Angles at a Point

When several angles meet at a single point, they form a complete circle.
The sum of all angles around a point is always 360°.
Angles at a point = 360°
This is because a full rotation makes a complete circle.
Example: If three angles meet at a point measuring 120°, 95°, and 80°, the fourth angle would be:
360° - 120° - 95° - 80° = 65°
2

Angles on a Straight Line

Angles that form a straight line are called supplementary angles.
The sum of angles on any straight line is always 180°.
Angles on a straight line = 180°
This property is used frequently in solving geometric problems.
Real-world example: When a ladder leans against a wall, the angles between the ladder, wall, and ground form straight line relationships.
If one angle on a straight line is 115°, the other must be 180° - 115° = 65°
3

Angles in Triangles

One of the most important facts in geometry:
Sum of angles in a triangle = 180°
This is true for ALL triangles, regardless of their shape or size.
Types of triangles:
• Equilateral: All sides equal, all angles = 60°
• Isosceles: Two sides equal, two base angles equal
• Scalene: All sides and angles different
• Right-angled: One angle = 90°
Example: In a triangle with angles 55° and 65°, the third angle is 180° - 55° - 65° = 60°
4

Angles in Quadrilaterals

A quadrilateral is any 4-sided shape.
The sum of interior angles in any quadrilateral is always 360°.
Sum of angles in a quadrilateral = 360°
Special quadrilaterals:
• Square/Rectangle: All angles = 90°
• Parallelogram: Opposite angles equal, adjacent angles supplementary
• Rhombus: Like parallelogram with all sides equal
• Trapezium: One pair of parallel sides
In a parallelogram, if one angle is 70°, the opposite is also 70°, and adjacent angles are 110°
5

Vertically Opposite Angles

When two straight lines cross, they create two pairs of vertically opposite angles.
Vertically opposite angles are ALWAYS equal.
Vertically opposite angles are equal
This is a very useful property when solving problems with intersecting lines.
Example: If two lines cross and one angle is 120°, then:
• The angle vertically opposite is also 120°
• The adjacent angles are both 60° (because 180° - 120° = 60°)
This property is often used with angles on a straight line to solve for unknown angles.

LCA Maths Application

In LCA Maths, angle facts are applied to real-world situations:
• Construction and carpentry
• Design and technical graphics
• Surveying and navigation
• Sports and recreation planning
Remember the key facts:
1. Angles at a point: 360°
2. Angles on a straight line: 180°
3. Angles in a triangle: 180°
4. Angles in a quadrilateral: 360°
5. Vertically opposite angles: Equal
Practice identifying which angle fact applies to solve real-world problems efficiently.
