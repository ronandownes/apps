Equation of a Circle

Simplest Case: Circle at the Origin

When the circle is centred at the origin (0, 0), the horizontal distance is simply x and the vertical distance is simply y.

By Pythagoras' Theorem:

x² + y² = r²

This is the equation of a circle with centre (0, 0) and radius r

x² + y² = r²
Centre: (0, 0)Radius: r

General Case: Circle at (h, k)

Now we translate the circle away from the origin...

When the centre is at (h, k), the horizontal distance becomes x − h and the vertical distance becomes y − k.

By Pythagoras' Theorem:

(x − h)² + (y − k)² = r²

Standard Form of a Circle

(x − h)² + (y − k)² = r²
Centre: (h, k)Radius: r

Transformations from x² + y² = r²

h → Horizontal translation (moves centre left/right)

k → Vertical translation (moves centre up/down)

r → Dilation (scales the circle larger/smaller)
