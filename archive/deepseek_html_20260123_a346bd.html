Official specification
Student-friendly version

MCS.1

Reason mathematically about problems

Students should be able to:

a.OFFICIALMake sense of a given problem and represent it using mathematics
a.STUDENTUnderstand what a problem is asking and turn it into maths
b.OFFICIALApply knowledge and skills to solve a problem, including decomposing it into manageable parts and/or simplifying it using appropriate assumptions
b.STUDENTBreak problems into smaller steps and solve them using maths skills
c.OFFICIALInterpret and justify their solution in terms of the original problem and communicate their findings mathematically
c.STUDENTExplain your answer in plain English and show how it solves the original problem

MCS.2

Calculations and operations

Students should be able to:

a.OFFICIALPerform calculations on positive and negative numbers: addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, square roots, and positive whole number powers
a.STUDENTWork with positive and negative numbers using all four operations, square roots, and powers
b.OFFICIALUse the order of arithmetic operations, including the use of brackets
b.STUDENTUse BIDMAS/BODMAS (order of operations) correctly, including brackets
c.OFFICIALPresent answers to the degree of accuracy required (nearest whole number, nearest thousand, two decimal places)
c.STUDENTRound answers to the required level of accuracy (whole numbers, thousands, decimals)
d.OFFICIALUse appropriate units and convert between them (mm, cm, m, km, seconds, minutes, hours, days, €k, €million, degrees)
d.STUDENTChoose correct units and convert between them (length, time, money, angles)
e.OFFICIALFlexibly convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
e.STUDENTMove confidently between fractions, decimals, and percentages
f.OFFICIALUse and understand ratio and proportion
f.STUDENTUse ratio and proportion to compare quantities and solve problems

MCS.3

Investigate 2D and 3D shapes

Students should be able to:

a.OFFICIALDraw and interpret scaled diagrams, using geometric tools (ruler, straight edge, set square, protractor, compass)
a.STUDENTDraw and understand scale diagrams using proper drawing tools
b.OFFICIALDraw and interpret nets, including rectangular solids and cylinders
b.STUDENTDraw and understand nets of 3D shapes like boxes and cylinders
c.OFFICIALFind the perimeter and area of 2D shapes (combinations of discs, triangles, and rectangles)
c.STUDENTCalculate the perimeter and area of flat shapes made from basic shapes
d.OFFICIALFind the volume and surface area of 3D shapes (rectangular solids and cylinders)
d.STUDENTCalculate the volume and surface area of 3D shapes like boxes and cylinders
e.OFFICIALRecognise and use angle facts: at a point, on a straight line, and in squares, rectangles, parallelograms, and triangles
e.STUDENTUse angle rules for points, straight lines, and common shapes
f.OFFICIALApply the theorem of Pythagoras to solve simple problems
f.STUDENTUse Pythagoras' theorem to solve right-angled triangle problems

MCS.4

Explore relationships and expressions

Students should be able to:

a.OFFICIALEvaluate expressions given the value of variables
a.STUDENTSubstitute numbers into formulas to find answers
b.OFFICIALRepresent linear relationships in tables, graphs, and generalised expressions (expressed in words)
b.STUDENTShow straight-line relationships in tables, graphs, and word formulas
c.OFFICIALSelect and use suitable strategies for finding solutions to problems involving linear relationships (graphic, numeric, trial and improvement, working backwards)
c.STUDENTUse different methods (graphs, tables, trial and error) to solve problems with straight-line patterns

MCS.5

Carry out a statistical investigation

Students should be able to:

a.OFFICIALGenerate a statistical question
a.STUDENTAsk a good question that can be answered with data
b.OFFICIALPlan and implement a method to generate and/or source unbiased, representative data
b.STUDENTCollect fair and representative data for your investigation
c.OFFICIALSelect, draw, and interpret appropriate graphical displays (bar charts, pie charts, trend graphs, histograms)
c.STUDENTChoose, draw and understand suitable charts and graphs for your data
d.OFFICIALSelect, calculate, and interpret summary statistics: measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and spread (range)
d.STUDENTCalculate and explain averages (mean, median, mode) and range
e.OFFICIALEvaluate the effectiveness of different graphical displays in representing data
e.STUDENTJudge which type of graph best shows your data clearly
f.OFFICIALDiscuss misconceptions and misuses of statistics
f.STUDENTRecognize when statistics are misleading or used incorrectly
